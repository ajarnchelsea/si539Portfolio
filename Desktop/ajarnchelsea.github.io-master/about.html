ABOUT CHELSEA

I'm a second year master's student at the University of Michigan School of Information, where I study human-computer interaction.

I'm particularly interested in designing computational hardware systems that are driven by gesture and voice interactions.

Previously, I studied psychology, film studies, and music at Bryn Mawr College, then taught English in Bangkok for three years. I discovered interaction design while working as a photo assistant in New York City, and realized that it sat at the intersection of my interests in human cognition and behavior, digital media, and education.

At UMSI, I've been able to develop as a UX professional while engaging in the passions that brought me here in the first place. I teach qualitative research skills to master's students as a Graduate Student Instructor. I'm designing systems that support musicians and filmmakers.
